UQ Attacking Corners

27 Mar 2014

Videos from last year of UQ's attacking corners

Straight flick into the top of the net on the keepers glove side.

  • The only practical way to stop this is for the first runner to get a stick (or body) on the ball. The postie and keeper can't cover this.

Straight flick low to the keepers stick side goes in.

  • This one was a poor miss by the keeper. But it goes to show that if you get the shot on target you are half a chance at a goal.
  • Last year they successfully trapped corners on our turf even though the pushouts were bumpy.
  • Hawley, Sam and Nick, have a look at where our runners go for this corner. This is NOT what we want. The second runner and stick side postie should be much closer to the net looking for the rebound.

Straight flick high to the keepers stick side. Goes in for a goal.

  • Rintala and Hathway can both flick high or low, left or right and they don't miss very often.
  • We need our first runner to put a lot of pressure on their flickers and trapper. They didn't run many variations last year (none on tape) so we can expect a lot of straight flicks.

Straight flick low is saved by the keepers feet. Postie has a chance to clear but doesn't push it hard enough. Uni forward has a shot and scores.

  • This shows the importance of a good clearance. Defenders, if you stay low you usually have enough time to get your stick on the ball, set your feet and fling the ball over the sideline. This is a skill that should be practiced.

From the games on tape we can see that they back their flickers a lot. No recorded variations or deflections. Their push out and trap are usually clean no matter the turf and their shots are mostly on target if they get past the runners. Their flickers tend to favour the stick side. Both flickers always flicked straight off the trapper e.g. no layoff to L1 that I saw.
