Easts Attacking Corners

19 Mar 2014

Videos from last year of Easts attacking corner battery

Corner against Commercial that resulted in a goal. Straight flick from L1 battery to keepers glove goes in after deflection off the keeper.

  • If a flick is fast enough it doesn't matter too much where it is aimed at.

Corner against Saints. Straight flick from L1 battery. Goes low right and beats the postie and keeper

  • This is how most of Easts corner goals come about. Low flicks to the keepers glove side
  • Webber and Ghodes both aim here
  • Josh, this one is on you. The shots are fast but almost always flat

Corner against Kedron. Pushout is wide forcing the trapper to stand up and run the ball to the right. Low hit is deflected from in front of the postie

  • If the pushout goes astray the trapper tends to run it to the right and shoot (rather than pass as in this example)
  • If the trapper does shoot he tends to hit it towards the keepers stick side
  • Sam, as the second runner you need to cover this play and be able to get to the trapper

Corner against Commercial that is saved. Straight flick from L1 battery is saved by the keeper. Shot is high to the keepers stick side.

  • Keepers were much better at saving the high shots. I couldn't find a video of a high flick from Webber going in last year
  • Interesting that Commercial leave two players on the backline, one on each post

Here is the summary of all the videos that are up on Youtube. You can see that Easts flick at the postie a lot and that they are quite effective at it
