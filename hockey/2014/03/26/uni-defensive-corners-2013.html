UQ Defensive Corners

Videos from last year of UQ's defensive corners

straight flick is saved by the keeper. The rebound is pounced on and another corner awarded.

  • Their second runner and stick side postie are very lazy on the rebound
  • Good work by Jenno and Kreisy to get in and force another corner
  • Josh, Look at Sam who is the L1 flicker in this clip. It is obvious that he is not going to get the ball. No follow through after the flick either to be in a position to trap a rebound.

Straight flick at the postie down low is a goal.

  • Hawley, Just a little bit of elevation is enough to put the postie off sometimes.
  • Josh, If you keep your stick straight and get your body behind the line then these low shots that are off the ground are easier to trap.

Straight flick high keepers stick side is saved. The rebound is regathered for a second shot which is also saved. The rebound is regathered again with the shot saved again and this time cleared.

  • Uni have a good keeper. He will make a lot of saves from corners. But their ability to clear the ball afterwards is poor. If we get in front of them then we will get first go at any rebounds.
  • The Bulimba player that gets the second shot rushes it too much. Keepers predict the hurried second shot because forwards often panic when they get the ball that close to the line. If the forward had just held his stick on the ball the keeper would have swung wildly at it and given away another corner. If he had of faked the shot and then slipped the ball to Andrew Neumann on the left post then they may have scored.
