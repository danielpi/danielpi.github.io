Speed and Deception Corners

17 Jan 2014

I want to start off with a pair of corners that work well together.

Speed

First up the straight flick from the top. The main strength of this corner is that it requires the least number of actions in order to get a shot on goal. Pushout, Trap, Shoot. This gives the defence the least time to prepare and gives the lowest number of opportunities for the attack to make a mistake. However it is also the corner that defences devote the most amount of effort to defend. The goalkeeper, postie and first runner set their positions so as to best defend the straight flick.

Deception

The complimentary corner is the layoff to L1. The main strength of this corner is that it looks identical to the straight flick up until the shot. This deception of the defence removes the first and second runners from the equation and also shifts the keeper to the left. It also gives the flicker more time and less pressure than when the shot is straight from the top of the circle. The key to this corner is that it must look identical to the straight flick. If the defence pick the layoff the flicker ends up being under a lot of pressure due to the time it takes to pass the ball from the trapper.

These two corners feed off each other. If the defence know that a layoff is a threat they will hedge their bets leading to less pressure on the straight flick. Likewise if the defence are worried about the straight flick they will over commit leaving themselves vulnerable to being tricked by the layoff. Teams that can execute both corners reliably, quickly and accurately will find that they are more successfull than teams that only focus on one or the other of these two corners.

Hunting for a Rebound

Well executed corners are a sure fire means of getting a shot on goal. That is not their only benefit though. They also give the attack 5-6 seconds of time with a numerical advantage over the defence and the ball in the circle. In order to exploit this the attack needs to pay attention to the most common rebound and clearence lines. A goalkepper that is at their limit trying to save a shot doesn't have many options for where they can clear the ball. Rebounders that get to the right spot will get plenty of opportunities to score.

All corners are a team effort

The quality of a corner battery is set by the ability of the weakest link. If anyone doesn't do thier job properly then the chance of a goal is greatly diminished. This next video shows a pitstop by the Ferrari F1 team. A group of 20 engineers change 4 tyres in 2 seconds. That is over a second quicker than the mid-tier teams can manage. When you watch it note how calm and composed the whole process is. Nobody is putting in a special effort or rushing their task. They are all just doing their own jobs at the speed and timing that they have practiced.

To give you an idea of what it takes to perfect a pitstop like the f1 teams do each of the teams does over 70 pitstop practices per race weekend. They also have practice rigs back at the factory so that the engineers can practice without the real car. Lots of repetition.
